On the verge of extinction,
Save the Sea turtles.





Sea turtles have contributed significantly to the upkeep of the balance and health of our marine environments for more than 100 million years. In addition to ensuring healthy coral reefs, they also deliver essential nutrients from the sea to the dunes along the coast.

Injurious human actions over the past 200 years have led to significant oceanic disturbances that have completely eliminated sea turtles from some regions of the world and brought them dangerously close to extinction in others.

The primary risks to these ancient reptiles are rampant poaching and over-exploitation, and they frequently become bycatch casualties. The species are negatively impacted by the fluctuating sand temperatures that their hatchlings depend on as a result of the more unpredictable global climate.
